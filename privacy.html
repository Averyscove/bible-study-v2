Privacy Policy

Starting with Christ — Community Edition. Last updated: 2026-05-07.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect when you use Starting with Christ (the "App"), how we use it, and the choices you have. We try to keep this in plain English. If anything is unclear, please reach out — contact details are at the bottom.

1. Who we are

Starting with Christ is a personal Bible study and community app. The App is operated by the developer listed in the App Store as the "Provider" (the "we," "us," or "our" in this policy).

2. What information we collect

Account information. When you create an account, we collect your email address and a password (the password is hashed and stored by Firebase Authentication; we never see it in plain text). We use these to let you sign in and to keep your data in sync across your devices.

Profile information. If you choose to use the community features, you provide a display name and optionally a short bio, a Bible-study experience level, and a list of books you enjoy. This information is visible to other signed-in users of the App.

Content you create. When you post in a discussion, send a chat message, reply to someone, or react to a post, that content is stored and displayed to other members of the relevant group. This includes the text you write, the time you wrote it, and your display name as it stood at the time of writing.

Reading data. The App tracks the chapters you've read, the personal reflection notes you write, the verses you bookmark, your reading streak, and how long you spend on each chapter. This information is private to you. It is stored locally on your device and synced to your account in our cloud database so it follows you across devices. No other user — including us, in normal operation — can read it.

Moderation data. If you flag a post, message, or reply for review, we record that you flagged it and when. If you block another user, we store their identifier in your private blocklist so we can hide their content from you.

Device-only data. The App stores some preferences only on your device and never sends them to our servers — currently this is the voice you've selected for read-aloud, your preferred font size, and your light/dark mode preference.

3. What information we do not collect

  • Real name (unless you choose to put it in your display name or bio).
  • Phone number.
  • Location.
  • Browsing history outside the App.
  • Contacts, photos, microphone, camera — the App does not request access to any of these.
  • Advertising or tracking identifiers.

4. How we use your information

  • To let you sign in and use the App.
  • To save your reading progress and notes so they sync across your devices.
  • To show your posts, messages, and reactions to other members of the groups you join.
  • To enforce moderation (review flagged content; honor your blocklist).
  • To respond to support requests.

We do not use your information for advertising. We do not sell or rent your information to anyone.

5. Who can see your information

  • Other signed-in users of the App can see your profile (display name, bio, level, favorite books) once you've created one.
  • Members of a group you've joined can see posts, messages, replies, and reactions you've published in that group.
  • The owner of a group you're a member of can see flags you've cast on content in that group, so they can review reports.
  • You, and only you, can see your reading progress, notes, bookmarks, streak, and blocklist.
  • We (the developer) can technically read database contents in the course of operating and supporting the App, but we do not access your private reading data unless you ask us to (for example, to help with a bug). We never read your private notes for product or marketing purposes.

6. Where your information is stored

Your data is stored in Google Firebase (Authentication and Cloud Firestore), which Google operates on its global infrastructure. Firebase encrypts data in transit and at rest. We use Firebase's standard security model and never embed credentials in the App that would allow access beyond your own account.

7. How long we keep your information

We keep your account information for as long as your account exists. If you delete your account using the "Delete my account" feature in the App, we permanently remove your profile, your blocklist, your group memberships, and the posts and chat messages you've authored. Replies you've written under other people's discussion posts are not auto-removed in this version of the App due to a technical limitation; we are working to close that gap. If you want those replies removed, please delete each one manually before deleting your account, or contact us and we will remove them for you.

8. Your rights and choices

  • Edit your profile: change your display name, bio, level, or favorite books at any time from the Community section.
  • Delete content: you can delete any post, message, or reply you've authored.
  • Block other users: hides their posts and messages from you everywhere in the App.
  • Leave a group: stop seeing it in your list at any time.
  • Delete your account: Settings → Account → Delete. This is final and immediate.
  • Use the App without an account: the reading plan, your reflection notes, and your bookmarks all work fully offline without any sign-in. Sign-in is only required for community features.
  • Request a copy of your data: contact us at the email below and we'll provide an export.

9. Children

The App is intended for users aged 13 and over. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you are a parent and believe your child under 13 has used the community features, please contact us and we will delete their account.

10. International users

The App is hosted on Google Firebase, which is operated globally. By using the App you understand that your information may be stored on servers outside your country of residence. If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or another region with applicable privacy laws (including the GDPR or California's CCPA), you have rights to access, correct, delete, and export your information; the in-App tools above implement most of these rights, and we will assist with any others on request.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we'll change the "Last updated" date at the top, and if the changes are significant we'll let you know inside the App. Continued use of the App after changes go into effect means you accept the updated policy.

12. Contact

If you have questions, requests, or concerns about your data, email us at the address listed in the App Store as the developer's support contact, or reach out to the developer through the GitHub repository where the App is hosted.

This document describes our practices in good faith. It is not a substitute for legal advice. If you operate in a jurisdiction with specific privacy law requirements (GDPR, CCPA, PIPEDA, etc.) and need a more formal version, contact the developer.
